Open Research

Research Experiences

I am currently involved in two research projects.

University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign • Additive Combinatorics and Discrete Mathematics

I am supervised and mentored by Professor József Balogh at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where I am collaborating on a research paper in additive combinatorics and discrete mathematics. Our project focuses on improving the best known upper bound for the maximum size of a Sidon set. This area involves advanced algebra and applied mathematics. Professor Balogh and his colleagues established an upper bound of about 0.998 through rigorous hand proofs, while previous UIUC students achieved a tighter bound using computational methods. My goal is to combine hand verification and computation to prove an even stronger bound than the current best result. To support this, I am developing new theorems and lemmas as part of the proof.

National University of Singapore • Planned Collaboration in Statistical Inference, Combinatorial Inference, Computational Applied Mathematics

I have also begun discussions about a second research collaboration with Professor Shuting Shen at the National University of Singapore. Our planned research will involve combinatorial inference, statistical inference, and computational applied mathematics. We have not finalized a specific problem or abstract yet, but we expect to begin formal work in January 2026.
